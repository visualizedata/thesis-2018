Observing Economic Insecurity near
The New School



THOUSANDS of people traverse the streets surrounding The New School each day. 14th street is a major commercial artery which hosts New Yorkers moving towards or away from the train entrances at Union Square or 6th Avenue. But not everyone is on the move: dotting the map are stories of the economically insecure population which spend their days amongst the more fortunate, hoping to experience our generosity but rarely drawing our gaze.




I tracked this population from Sept 11 through Sept 15 and discretely logged several data points including address, date, time, gender and a description of the individual. I sought to only collect information on individuals within a .3 mile radius from The New School's University Center on the streets I typically traverse. I specifically omitted anyone who was vending, entertaining or performing services.




The purpose of tracking this population is to quantify their plight while humanizing their condition in a way which is difficult to do during the usual chaos and carryings-on of the day. Additionally, I'm interested in expanding the data set and observing whether there are any patterns related to gender.
